## 1. FUNDAMENTAL ANALYSIS RULES (The Filter)
*If the company does not pass these filters, the technical setup is irrelevant.*

### 1.1. PER (Price to Earnings Ratio) Valuation
* **The Golden Rule:** **NEVER** recommend buying a company with a PER > 50. This is statistically dangerous.
* **Historical Comparison:** Compare Current PER vs. 5-Year Average PER.
    * **Buy Signal:** Ideally, Current PER should be close to **50% of the historical minimum** or significantly below the 5-year average.
    * **Range:** Look for PER 10-20 for stable companies.
* **Trend:** A falling PER is good *only* if earnings are stable/growing (bargain). If earnings are falling faster than price, it's a "value trap."

### 1.2. Financial Health (Debt & Cash)
* **The "Cotton Test" (Debt/FCF):**
    * Calculate: Years to pay off Total Debt using Free Cash Flow (FCF).
    * **Optimal:** < 3-4 years.
    * **Red Flag:** "Criminal Debt" (High Debt, Low/Negative FCF, Low Cash).
* **Cash Position:** Net Cash position is highly favorable.

### 1.3. Growth & Margins
* **Revenue & Earnings:** Look for sustained Year-over-Year (YoY) growth.
* **Margin Compression:** **Alert:** If Revenues rise but Net Income falls, margins are compressing. This is a negative signal.
* **Stagnation:** If a "growth" stock flatlines in revenue for 2-3 quarters, expect severe punishment (-40%).

## 2. TECHNICAL ANALYSIS RULES (The Trigger)
*Technicals decide "When" and "Where" to buy.*

### 2.1. Moving Averages (MA)
* **MA200 (200-Day Moving Average) is KING.**
    * **Buy Zone:** **Below** or **Touching** the MA200.
    * **Avoid:** Do NOT buy if price is far above the MA200 ("Maximum Separation"). Wait for reversion to the mean.
    * **"Knife Catching":** Good companies far from MA200 will eventually fall sharply ("like a knife") to touch it.

### 2.2. Fibonacci Retracements
* **Entry Zones:**
    * **38.2%:** First entry for very strong trends.
    * **50% - 61.8% (Golden Pocket):** Ideal entry zone after a rally.
* **Take Profit Zones:** Extensions at **138.2%** and **161.8%**.

### 2.3. Structures
* **Gaps:** Price tends to fill gaps. Use old gaps as price targets (magnets).
* **Triangles:** Watch for symmetrical triangle breakouts.

## 3. RISK MANAGEMENT
* **Tiered Entries:** Never invest 100% at once. Enter in chunks (e.g., 30% at MA200, 30% at Fib 61.8%).
* **Position Sizing:** Small amounts for speculative plays (Plug Power types). Larger for solid compounders (Airbus, Inditex).
